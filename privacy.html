Privacy Policy

TABULARY · LAST UPDATED 2026-07-18

Tabulary is a browser extension for saving and organizing open tabs. This page explains what data it handles, where it goes, and what control you have over it.

The short version

  • By default, everything you save stays on your device. No account, no server, no tracking.
  • If you choose to sign in with Google, your saved tabs and a small profile file (display name, optional photo, theme) are backed up to a private, hidden folder in your own Google Drive — never to any server we operate.
  • We don't run analytics, don't show ads, and don't sell or share your data with anyone.

Sign-in and Google Drive access

Signing in is optional. Tabulary offers two ways:

  • Email and password — creates an account for identity only. Your tabs stay on the device unless you also connect Google.
  • Google sign-in — also requests one additional, narrow permission: drive.appdata. This lets Tabulary read and write only its own hidden data folder in your Google Drive. It cannot see, list, or modify any of your actual files, photos, or documents in Drive.

You can revoke this access anytime from Google Account → Security → Third-party apps, or delete the stored data directly from Google Drive → Settings → Manage apps → Tabulary → "Delete hidden app data."

What we don't do

  • We don't run our own servers that store your tab data.
  • We don't use analytics, ad networks, or tracking pixels.
  • We don't sell, rent, or share your data with third parties.
  • We don't read the content of your actual browsing beyond the tabs you explicitly choose to save.

Browser permissions Tabulary requests

PermissionWhy
tabsTo read the title/URL of your currently open tabs when you choose to save them.

Deleting your data

You can export everything as a JSON file, clear your local data, clear your account's cloud data, or delete your account entirely — all from the extension's Settings → Data tab.

Children's privacy

Tabulary is not directed at children and we do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 13.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the "Last updated" date above will change accordingly.
